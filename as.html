24/7 Sexual Assault Crisis Hotline

We are here to help all sexual assault survivors—whether the attack took place today or 30 years ago. You can call our sexual assault hotline 24 hours a day, seven days a week, 365 days a year for immediate assistance.

Crisis Intervention and Safety Planning

Crisis intervention is an immediate and short-term emergency response to mental, emotional, physical, and behavioral distress. Crisis interventions help restore an individual’s equilibrium to their biopsychosocial functioning and minimize the potential for long-term trauma or distress.

A safety plan is a personalized and practical plan that helps identify things a victim can do to better protect themselves and their children and to help reduce the risk of being hurt.

24/7 Medical Accompaniment to Hospitals

The main focus of accompaniment, as an Advocate, is to stay by the survivor’s side and provide support throughout their entire journey in the emergency room, walking them through any questions they may have throughout the process.

Advocacy/Case Management

Our Case Managers are certified Rape Crisis Counselors and Domestic Violence Advocates. Case Managers collaborate with survivors to provide support, resources, and guidance in decision-making, working to ensure that safety is paramount. Our team uses a trauma-informed approach and believes that each survivor is the expert of their own life.

Victim Information and Notification Everyday (VINE)

VINE is the nation’s leading victim notification network. It allows survivors, victims of crime, and other concerned citizens to access timely and reliable information about offenders or criminal cases in U.S. jails and prisons. Sexual Assault Support Groups

Support groups can provide encouragement, comfort, and advice. These support groups are provided at no costs for victims and survivors, partners of vicitms and survivors, and non-offending parents of children who have been sexually abused.

Legal Services: Civil Remedies for Sexual Assualt

Centers provide civil legal services, free of charge, to victims of family and/or sexual violence. These services include direct representation in court, pro se assistance, in depth counsel and advice, and the provision of basic legal information.

Crime Victim's Compensation

The Crime Victims’ Compensation (CVC) Program helps crime victims and their immediate families with the financial costs of crime. CVC covers crime-related costs such as counseling, medical treatment, funerals, and loss of income not paid by other sources.

Info from Texas Attorney General

Address Confidentiality Program (ACP)

The Address Confidentiality Program (ACP) provides a substitute post office box address and mail forwarding service. This helps prevent your actual address from being revealed in state government documents and files while still allowing you to receive and send mail.
